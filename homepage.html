Mission

The Data Schools Framework

A decentralized, cross-disciplinary approach to ethical AI governance

Globalist Perspective

Focuses on global cooperation and long-term sustainability of AI solutions for humanity at large, represented by Elysium (Violet).

Nationalist Perspective

Prioritizes local needs and values of individual nations, ensuring AI respects national identity, represented by Abzimuth (Teal).

Populist Perspective

Ensures AI aligns with public desires and societal good, emphasizing accessibility and accountability, represented by Ocidentica (Taupe).

Checks & Balances System

Ranked Choice Voting

Ensures all viewpoints are considered in decision-making through transparent voting processes.

Multi-Tiered Oversight

Data changes are scrutinized at teacher, professor, and principal levels before implementation.

Continuous Feedback

Ongoing evaluation of AI impacts and governance effectiveness with stakeholder input.

Data Schools Structure

Multi-ideological organizations for AI training and governance

Educational Framework

Data Teachers

  • Identify and flag biases in datasets (cultural, geographical, contextual)
  • Implement ethical guidelines in AI training processes
  • Maintain data accuracy and understand societal implications

Data Professors

  • Design experiments to assess AI model performance
  • Engage in advanced research to improve governance frameworks
  • Mentor teachers in bias detection and ethical standards

The Three Principals

Elysium (Violet)

Globalist Ideology

  • Prioritizes global cooperation and sustainability
  • Focuses on long-term AI solutions for humanity
  • Ensures AI benefits are distributed equitably worldwide

Abzimuth (Teal)

Nationalist Ideology

  • Protects national identity and autonomy
  • Ensures AI respects local values and traditions
  • Addresses specific national needs and priorities

Ocidentica (Taupe)

Populist Ideology

  • Represents public desires and societal good
  • Emphasizes accessibility and transparency
  • Ensures AI remains accountable to the people

Ethical Oversight Process

1. Proposal Submission

Teachers submit data change proposals with bias analysis and ethical considerations.

2. Professor Review

Professors conduct simulations and deeper analysis of proposed changes.

3. Principal Deliberation

Principals from all three ideologies evaluate the proposal through ranked choice voting.

4. Implementation & Feedback

Approved changes are implemented with continuous monitoring and public feedback.
